## Do Women Lose Weight Slower Than Men?

In many mixed-sex weight-loss programs, men often show greater scale weight loss - especially early on. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Christensen et al., 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6282840/)) Part of this is real (men often lose more kilograms), but part is arithmetic (men often start heavier and have higher energy expenditure). ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) When weight loss is expressed as a percentage of initial body weight or when energy deficits are tightly controlled, sex differences shrink and sometimes disappear. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Caudwell et al., 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22895380/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))

### What people mean by “lose weight slower”

- **Absolute weight loss (kg/week):** men often lose more because they have higher baseline mass and energy expenditure. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))
    
- **Relative weight loss (% of starting weight):** differences are smaller and often not statistically meaningful. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))
    
- **Fat loss vs scale weight:** men may lose more total weight, but the composition can differ; in some datasets men lose a higher fraction of weight loss as fat-free/lean mass, which implies women may lose relatively more as fat **(inference)**. ([Das et al., 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/); [Aronica et al., 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7752762/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/ "Body-composition changes in the Comprehensive Assessment of Long-term Effects of Reducing Intake of Energy (CALERIE)-2 study: a 2-y randomized controlled trial of calorie restriction in nonobese humans - PubMed"))
    
- **“Stalled” scale weight:** women have cyclical fluid shifts that can mask fat loss over short windows **(inference)**. ([Kanellakis et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/ "Changes in body weight and body composition during the menstrual cycle - PubMed"))
    

### What the best evidence shows

A systematic review of randomized weight-loss interventions found that studies including both sexes often report greater weight loss in men, but the overall difference is not huge and the authors concluded there is little evidence that men and women require fundamentally different strategies. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/)) The same review also noted that, in some interventions, adjusting for baseline weight removed apparent sex differences in weight-loss outcomes. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))

In trials with tightly prescribed energy deficits, men often still lose more absolute weight, but differences narrow when outcomes are normalized to baseline body weight. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Christensen et al., 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6282840/)) For example, in the PREVIEW study’s 8-week low-energy diet phase (3.4 MJ / **810 kcal/day**), men lost **11.8%** of body weight vs **10.3%** in women. ([Christensen et al., 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6282840/)) Similarly, in a secondary analysis of the DIETFITS randomized trial, mean percentage weight loss at 6 months was reported as similar between sexes. ([Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))

In supervised exercise interventions where energy expenditure is controlled, sex differences in fat loss can disappear. ([Caudwell et al., 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22895380/)) A 12-week supervised exercise program (≈**10.5 MJ/week**) produced significant fat mass reductions in both sexes with no between-sex differences in fat mass or percent body fat change. ([Caudwell et al., 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22895380/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22895380/ "No sex difference in body fat in response to supervised and measured exercise - PubMed"))

### Why men often lose more weight in kilograms

Energy expenditure scales with body size and lean mass. ([Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) In a baseline analysis from CALERIE, non-obese men had **~20%** higher total daily energy expenditure than women (a mean difference of **~580 kcal/day**). ([Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) The same paper reports resting energy expenditure in men was **~22% higher** than in women (a mean difference of **~290 kcal/day**). ([Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/ "Energy requirements in nonobese men and women: results from CALERIE1 - PMC"))

If two people follow the same “diet plan” in absolute calories (e.g., both eat 1500 kcal/day), the person with higher expenditure will have the bigger deficit and typically lose faster **(inference from energy balance arithmetic)**. ([Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) This makes “men lose faster” look like a biological inevitability when it is often a mismatch in deficit size **(inference)**. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/ "Energy requirements in nonobese men and women: results from CALERIE1 - PMC"))

Even in controlled deficits, body composition differs. ([Das et al., 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) In the CALERIE calorie restriction trial, men lost significantly more trunk fat and a higher fraction of their weight loss as fat-free mass than women. ([Das et al., 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/)) That can change how the scale moves and how bodies look **(inference)**. ([Das et al., 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28228420/ "Body-composition changes in the Comprehensive Assessment of Long-term Effects of Reducing Intake of Energy (CALERIE)-2 study: a 2-y randomized controlled trial of calorie restriction in nonobese humans - PubMed"))

### Early losses and water weight

Early in dieting, some “fast loss” is glycogen and the water stored with it. ([Kreitzman et al., 1992](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/)) Glycogen is stored in hydrated form (roughly **3-4 parts water** per part glycogen), so depleting glycogen can drop scale weight quickly without reflecting fat loss. ([Kreitzman et al., 1992](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/ "Glycogen storage: illusions of easy weight loss, excessive weight regain, and distortions in estimates of body composition - PubMed"))

Because men often have more lean mass, total glycogen (and associated water) could be larger in absolute terms, which can plausibly contribute to larger early water-linked drops **(inference)**. ([Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/); [Kreitzman et al., 1992](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/ "Glycogen storage: illusions of easy weight loss, excessive weight regain, and distortions in estimates of body composition - PubMed"))

Key nuance: the glycogen–water mechanism explains why early scale drops can occur without fat loss, but it does not by itself prove a consistent sex difference in “water weight loss” **(inference)**. ([Kreitzman et al., 1992](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/); [Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1615908/ "Glycogen storage: illusions of easy weight loss, excessive weight regain, and distortions in estimates of body composition - PubMed"))

### Hormonal cycling and apparent plateaus

In reproductive-age women, the menstrual cycle can shift fluid retention and scale weight independent of fat mass. ([Kanellakis et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/)) In one study, body weight was higher during menstruation by **0.450 kg**, attributed to an increase of **0.474 kg** in extracellular water (≈0.5 kg overall). ([Kanellakis et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/ "Changes in body weight and body composition during the menstrual cycle - PubMed"))

This effect is big enough to hide a typical week of fat loss for some people, making progress appear slower if you compare weigh-ins across different cycle phases **(inference)**. ([Kanellakis et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/ "Changes in body weight and body composition during the menstrual cycle - PubMed"))

### Practical interpretation

- Men often lose more weight in kilograms, especially early, largely because they tend to start heavier and burn more calories at baseline. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Redman et al., 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3862460/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))
    
- When you compare percent weight loss or match the actual energy deficit, men and women appear more similar than “kg/week” anecdotes suggest. ([Williams et al., 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/); [Caudwell et al., 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22895380/); [Christensen et al., 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6282840/); [Kim et al., 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12910376/)) ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4359685/ "Effectiveness of weight loss interventions – is there a difference between men and women: a systematic review - PMC"))
    
- Women’s scale weight can be noisier due to cycle-related fluid shifts, so trend weight over weeks (or compare same-cycle-phase weigh-ins) to avoid false plateaus. ([Kanellakis et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/)) ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37395124/ "Changes in body weight and body composition during the menstrual cycle - PubMed"))
