# Dermatology Curriculum

## 1. What Is Skin Actually Doing?

- Why does the body need skin beyond "covering"?
- What is the skin barrier physically, chemically, and immunologically?
- How do keratinocytes, melanocytes, fibroblasts, immune cells, nerves, vessels, glands, follicles, and microbiota cooperate?
- Why do the face, scalp, armpits, groin, palms, soles, lips, nails, and eyelids behave differently?
- How does skin heal after injury?
- Why does inflammation leave pigment, scars, texture change, or keloids?
- How does skin age biologically?

## 2. How Do Dermatologists See?

- What is the difference between a macule, patch, papule, plaque, nodule, vesicle, bulla, pustule, wheal, cyst, scale, crust, erosion, ulcer, fissure, atrophy, and lichenification?
- Why does distribution matter: flexural, extensor, follicular, seborrheic, acral, dermatomal, photodistributed, generalized?
- How does the same disease look different across light, brown, and dark skin?
- What can be diagnosed visually, and what requires scraping, culture, biopsy, dermoscopy, or labs?
- How does histopathology explain what the eye sees?

## 3. Why Does Skin Become Inflamed?

- What is inflammation trying to do?
- Why does inflamed skin itch, burn, swell, scale, ooze, crack, thicken, or change color?
- Why does eczema happen?
- Why does psoriasis form thick plaques?
- Why do hives appear and disappear?
- Why does allergic contact dermatitis appear after a delay?
- Why does rosacea affect the central face?
- Why do topical steroids help, and how can they harm?
- Why do biologics work in psoriasis and eczema?
- When is "just a rash" medically dangerous?

## 4. Why Does Acne Happen?

- Why do comedones form?
- Why does sebum matter?
- Is acne bacterial, hormonal, inflammatory, or keratinization-driven?
- Why do retinoids work?
- Why does benzoyl peroxide matter?
- Why are antibiotics limited?
- How does isotretinoin work?
- What is hormonal acne?
- When is acne not acne: folliculitis, rosacea, perioral dermatitis, steroid acne, fungal folliculitis?
- Why does acne scar?
- Why does acne leave post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, especially in brown skin?
- What is the evidence on dairy, sugar, whey, stress, sleep, gut health, and supplements?

## 5. Why Does Pigment Change?

- What is melanin doing biologically?
- Why do acne marks become dark?
- Why does melasma happen?
- Why does vitiligo happen?
- Why can inflammation cause hyperpigmentation or hypopigmentation?
- Why do UV and visible light matter?
- What is tanning?
- How do hydroquinone, azelaic acid, retinoids, vitamin C, niacinamide, tranexamic acid, peels, and lasers work?
- Why is pigment difficult to treat?
- Which "brightening" claims are misleading?

## 6. Why Does Hair Grow, Shed, Miniaturize, or Scar?

- What is the hair cycle?
- Why does androgenetic alopecia miniaturize hair?
- Why does telogen effluvium happen after stress, illness, dieting, childbirth, or deficiency?
- Why does alopecia areata cause patches?
- Why does traction alopecia matter mechanically and culturally?
- What are scarring alopecias, and why are they urgent?
- How do minoxidil, finasteride, dutasteride, anti-androgens, PRP, ketoconazole, and light therapy work?
- Which hair supplement claims are weak?

## 7. Why Do Infections Look So Different on Skin?

- Why does fungus form rings?
- Why do bacteria produce pus, crust, cellulitis, or abscesses?
- Why do viruses cause blisters, grouped lesions, warts, or molluscum?
- Why does scabies itch intensely at night?
- Why do steroids make fungal infections worse?
- When are antibiotics necessary?
- Why do recurrent skin infections happen?
- How do KOH prep, culture, swabs, biopsy, and clinical pattern recognition fit together?

## 8. What Does Skin Reveal About Internal Disease?

- What skin signs point to diabetes, thyroid disease, liver disease, kidney disease, gut disease, autoimmune disease, nutritional deficiency, malignancy, or infection?
- How do drug eruptions happen?
- What is vasculitis?
- Why do some rashes indicate systemic inflammation?
- Which skin findings are clues to internal disease rather than isolated skin problems?

## 9. What Is Dangerous?

- What should never be casually self-treated?
- How do melanoma, basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, actinic keratosis, and benign lesions differ?
- What makes a mole suspicious?
- What is acral melanoma, and why does it matter in darker skin?
- What are SJS/TEN, DRESS, necrotizing infection, vasculitis, erythroderma, and rapidly progressive cellulitis?
- When does delay cause serious harm?

## 10. Why Does Skincare Work or Fail?

- What does cleansing remove, and what can it damage?
- What does a moisturizer actually do?
- Why do some people need occlusives while others break out?
- What makes sunscreen effective in real life?
- Why do retinoids irritate before they help?
- When is exfoliation useful, and when is it destructive?
- Which ingredients have strong evidence, weak evidence, or mostly marketing?
- How do formulation, concentration, vehicle, irritation, adherence, and skin type change the outcome?

## 11. How Do Treatments Actually Work?

- Why do topical steroids work?
- Why do potency, body site, age, duration, and frequency matter?
- Why do retinoids normalize skin behavior?
- Why do antifungals, antibiotics, antivirals, and antiparasitics differ?
- Why do biologics target specific cytokines?
- Why do lasers affect pigment, vessels, hair, or resurfacing differently?
- Why do some treatments require lab monitoring?
- Why does pregnancy change treatment choices?
- Why does the same active ingredient behave differently in a cream, gel, ointment, lotion, foam, wash, or serum?
